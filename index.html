Sigiriya

Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress situated in the northern Matale District of the Central Province in Sri Lanka. This site holds considerable historical and archaeological importance, characterized by a towering granite column that rises approximately 180 meters (590 feet) high. As per the ancient Sri Lankan chronicle, this region was initially a vast forest, which transformed into a hill due to storms and landslides. King Kashyapa (AD 477–495) chose this location for his new capital, constructing his palace atop the rock and adorning its surfaces with vibrant frescoes. Approximately halfway up the rock's face, he erected a grand entrance in the shape of a colossal lion, which inspired the name of the site: Sinhagiri, or Lion Rock. Following the king's demise, the capital and royal palace were deserted. The site subsequently served as a Buddhist monastery until the 14th century. Today, Sigiriya is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Unawatuna

Unawatuna is a coastal town located in the Galle district of Sri Lanka. It serves as a prominent tourist destination, renowned for its beautiful beach and vibrant coral reefs. As a suburb of Galle, Unawatuna boasts a rich biodiversity. One of its most significant eco-tourism attractions is the Kadolana marshland or mangrove, which has been partially dredged and filled to accommodate the development of a hotel for a German hotel chain. Ornithologist Clive Byers has recorded over sixty species of endemic birds in the area, including terns, egrets, herons, sandpipers, and kingfishers, along with rarer species such as the lesser whistling duck, the Asian palm swift, the white-breasted waterhen, Loten's sunbird, and the black bittern. These avian species are primarily observed in the remaining marshy regions and the Rumassala Hillock.

Ella

Ella is a charming village in Sri Lanka's highlands, famous for its lush tea estates, majestic mountains, and stunning waterfalls. It's a must-visit for travelers seeking breathtaking views and is a dream destination for trekkers with some of the island's best hiking trails. Recently, it has gained popularity among tourists. The misty mountain peaks and spectacular sunrises in Ella are unforgettable. After a morning hike, visitors can explore a waterfall, enjoy delicious Sri Lankan cuisine with a beer, relax, and then take a scenic rail walk to the iconic Nine Arch Bridge. This experience is sure to be cherished.Ella Rock in Sri Lanka is a must-visit destination. As you climb, you'll pass tea plantations, railway tracks, and eucalyptus forests. At the summit, enjoy breathtaking views of Ella Gap and Little Adam Peak, especially stunning at sunrise when the sky turns pink.

Sinharaja

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a significant forest reserve and a biodiversity hotspot located in Sri Lanka. It holds international importance and has been recognized as both a Biosphere Reserve and a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. This pristine rainforest, which is part of the Sri Lanka lowland rain forests ecoregion, has been largely protected from extensive commercial logging due to its difficult accessibility. It was designated as a World Biosphere Reserve in 1978 and received World Heritage Site status in 1988.The rainforest likely formed during the Jurassic period (from 200 million years to 145 million years ago). This forest encompasses an area of 36,000 hectares. Preliminary studies on the fauna of Sinharaja have revealed that there is a high degree of endemism among the butterflies, fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds and mammals. In fact 95%of the endemic birds of Sri Lanka are recorded in Sinharaja. Endemism among mammals and butterflies are also greater than 50%.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains, along with its surrounding areas, forests, and the adjacent peak wilderness, represents the most significant catchment area for nearly all major rivers in Sri Lanka. This region is renowned for its exceptional scenic beauty and conservation value, encompassing a variety of habitats. The climate of Horton Plains is characterized as a wet montane forest, with an average annual temperature ranging from 14 to 16 degrees Celsius and a relatively low humidity level of 65%. As a prominent tourist attraction, Horton Plains features World's End as its primary highlight. Additionally, Baker's Falls, a waterfall created by the Belihul Oya tributary of the Walawe River-bears the name of Sir Samuel Baker, a hunter and explorer who endeavored to establish a European agricultural settlement in Nuwara Eliya.

Nuwara Eliya

The city was founded in 1846 by Samuel Baker, an explorer recognized for his explorations of Lake Albert and the upper Nile. Nuwara Eliya's climate made it a perfect retreat for British civil servants and planters in Ceylon, earning it the nickname "Little England." This hill country escape provided British colonialists with opportunities for various leisure activities, such as fox hunting, deer hunting, elephant hunting, polo, golf, and cricket. Among the town's key attractions are the golf course, trout streams, Victoria Park, and options for boating or fishing on Lake Gregory. Victoria Park is a scenic and popular haven, especially appreciated by birdwatchers during quieter times due to the excellent chances of spotting various species, particularly the Indian blue robin, pied thrush, and scaly thrush, which are often found in the thicker underbrush. Additionally, the Kashmir flycatcher is another fascinating bird species that can be seen within the park.
